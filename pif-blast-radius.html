Why Other Protocols Got Hit

What is contagion in DeFi? In traditional finance, when a bank fails, other banks that lent to it or held its assets also take losses. DeFi works the same way. Many Solana protocols had deposited user funds into Drift's vaults to earn yield — lending, staking, and running trading strategies through Drift's infrastructure. When Drift's vaults were drained, every protocol with money inside those vaults lost it too. They didn't get hacked directly. They got caught in someone else's explosion.

Think of it like an apartment building. The attacker set fire to one unit. But everyone who stored valuables in that unit — neighbors, businesses, people renting storage — lost their property too. The fire wasn't aimed at them. But their money was in the building.

The ripple effect
Drift's TVL collapsed from $550M to $232M — a 58% drop. But the damage extended across the entire Solana DeFi ecosystem, which lost $700M in total TVL ($6.1B → $5.4B) as users pulled funds from other protocols out of fear. SOL briefly dipped to $83.82. The DRIFT token is down 51% and hasn't recovered. 35 DeFi protocols have been exploited in 2026 so far, with approximately $453M extracted — and the year isn't even half over.
