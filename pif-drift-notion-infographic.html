🔐 Why It Worked — The Two Problems

🧮The Math Problem
Every loop through the washing machine multiplies the number of transactions investigators must trace. Send $1M to 1,000 wallets. Each wallet forwards to 3 more wallets. That's 3,000 wallets. Run it again: 9,000 wallets. Again: 27,000. After just three loops through 50,000 wallets, the trail has millions of individual transactions. No team on earth can trace every one before the money moves again.
⏱️The Time Problem
Every minute the bots run = more wallets, more hops, more noise. The window to recover funds shrinks with every second. USDC can be frozen by Circle — but only if Circle acts before the attacker converts it. ETH cannot be frozen by anyone. The $42.6M already converted to ETH is effectively beyond centralized recovery. The attacker is racing to convert the remaining $66.7M USDC before a freeze. The bots are buying time.
